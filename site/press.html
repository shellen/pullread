About

Short (50 words)

Pull Read is a macOS menu bar app that saves articles from RSS feeds and bookmark services as local Markdown files. Highlight, annotate, summarize with AI, and listen with text-to-speech. Your reading library stays on your machine — searchable, portable, and never locked into a platform.

Long (100 words)

Pull Read is a free, open-source macOS app for people who read seriously. It pulls articles from RSS feeds, Instapaper, Pocket, Pinboard, and Raindrop.io, then saves them as clean Markdown files with YAML frontmatter. Every article can be highlighted, annotated, summarized by AI, and read aloud. A knowledge graph surfaces connections between articles, and weekly reviews help you keep up without falling behind. Built by Jason Shellen — who spent 20 years making the web more readable, from Google Reader to Blogger to the Atom format — Pull Read is designed for readers who want to think about what they read, not just consume it.

What Makes It Different

Local-first. Articles are Markdown files on your machine, not rows in someone else's database. Open them in Obsidian, Logseq, VS Code, or any text editor. No account, no cloud, no lock-in.

Reading as thinking. Highlights, annotations, notebooks, and AI summaries turn passive reading into active understanding. A knowledge graph connects articles by topic so ideas build on each other.

Privacy by design. No analytics, no telemetry, no tracking. Apple Intelligence summaries run entirely on-device. Third-party AI is opt-in with your own API keys.

Built by someone who's done this before. Jason Shellen helped create Google Reader, co-authored the Atom syndication format, and has been building tools for reading the web for two decades.
