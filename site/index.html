How it works

Three steps to your reading archive

1

Connect your feeds

Add RSS feeds from Instapaper, Pinboard, Raindrop, or any source. Paste a blog URL and Pull Read auto-discovers the feed.

2

Pull Read syncs

Articles are fetched, cleaned with Mozilla's Readability, and saved as markdown with rich metadata in YAML frontmatter.

3

Read and annotate

Browse in the built-in reader with highlights, notes, AI summaries, and text-to-speech. Or open files in Obsidian or any editor.

The Case for Local-First

Martin Kleppmann inkandswitch.com Feb 7, 2026
Summary Anthropic claude-sonnet-4-5-20250929

Explores why local-first software — keeping data on the device while enabling collaboration — offers better ownership, speed, and privacy than cloud-only apps.

Cloud apps have become the default, but they come with trade-offs that most people accept without question: latency, lock-in, and the assumption that your data lives on someone else's server.
Local-first software keeps your data on your device while still enabling collaboration. The idea is simple: you shouldn't need a server to use your own files.
