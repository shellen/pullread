How Do You Know What You Know?

4h ago
Summary

A reflection on how we acquire knowledge in the internet age, where information flows so freely that we rarely stop to examine the sources of our beliefs and understanding.

Apple Intelligence

We spend hours each day absorbing information from screens, but how often do we pause to consider where our knowledge actually comes from? The internet has made information abundant, but it hasn't made us better at evaluating what we consume. There's an old epistemological question that feels freshly urgent.

The philosopher Timothy Williamson argues that knowledge is more fundamental than belief — that we don't build knowledge from beliefs, but rather beliefs are what's left when knowledge breaks down. In a media landscape where every assertion competes for attention, this framing offers something useful.
